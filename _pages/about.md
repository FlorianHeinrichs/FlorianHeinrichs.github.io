I am Florian Heinrichs, Professor of Data Science & Statistics at the [**FH Aachen University of Applied Sciences**](https://www.fh-aachen.de/fachbereiche/medizintechnik-und-technomathematik). My research develops non-parametric statistical methods for time‑varying processes, focusing on **outlier** and **change detection** in **locally stationary time series** and inference in **functional data analysis**. I combine rigorous asymptotic theory with finite sample evaluation and reproducible software so that methods are both reliable and usable in practice.

I earned my PhD in Mathematical Statistics from the [**Ruhr University Bochum**]( https://math.ruhr-uni-bochum.de/) under the supervision of [Holger Dette]( https://math.ruhr-uni-bochum.de/fakultaet/arbeitsbereiche/stochastik/gruppe-dette/team/holger-dette/) and [Axel Bücher]( https://math.ruhr-uni-bochum.de/fakultaet/arbeitsbereiche/stochastik/gruppe-buecher/team/axel-buecher/). After my doctorate I spent three years in industry as Chief Data Scientist working on data-driven R&D in **neurotechnology**. Since returning to academia I have focused on bridging theoretical developments with applied implementations.

I teach courses in statistics, time series analysis, and data science, and I supervise MSc/BSc theses on topics such as change‑point and outlier detection and functional data analysis, and maintain implementations of my methods on GitHub.

I'm always eager to connect with fellow researchers in mathematical statistics. If you'd like to discuss potential collaborations, joint projects, or talks, feel free to [contact me](mailto:f.heinrichs@fh-aachen.de).
